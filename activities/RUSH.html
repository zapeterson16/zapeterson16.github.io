Team RUSH Robotics

FIRST is an organization that helps to promote science and technology. The FIRST Robotics Competition is a competition where a game is released in January, then teams build a 120 pound robot over the course of 6 weeks to compete with.

About Team RUSH

Team RUSH is a FIRST robotics team competing in the FIRST Robotics Competition. Team RUSH is one of the oldest teams in FIRST, with over 20 years of experience. In 2013, Team RUSH won the Global Chairman's Award which is the highest award in all of FIRST robotics. Team RUSH was also the Michigan state champion in 2014 and 2016, and a state finalist in 2015. Team RUSH was also a finalist in its division at the world competitions in 2014 and 2015. I was one of the two robot operators from 2014-2016.

My Contribution

Being at Team RUSH was how I spent almost all my free time in high school. A typical build season for me meant working every day except Sunday from after school until at least 9pm, and from 9am to 4pm on Saturday. When I started as a freshman, I jumped in on the programming sub-team and was the back-up robot operator. My sophomore year, I programmed the scouting app, which was our most successful scouting app to date. I also took on more of the software development, and was the robot operator for competitions. During my junior year, I was the captain of the programming sub-team, developed a majority of the software for the robot in LabVIEW, and was still the robot operator. My senior year, I was the team captain and wrote all of the software for the robot, with help from Wineman Technology on the autonomous software. Find out more about that here. I was also the robot operator for the third year.
